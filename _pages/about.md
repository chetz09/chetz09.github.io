I am a **Scientist at Harvard Medical School / Massachusetts General Hospital**, working on molecular and quantitative MRI for cancer and gene-therapy imaging. My research sits at the intersection of **MR physics, pulse-sequence programming, and machine learning** — I design quantitative imaging biomarkers that translate from preclinical scanners to the clinic.

Before joining Harvard/MGH, I was a Scientist at the **University of Wisconsin–Madison**, and before that a postdoc at **MD Anderson Cancer Center**. I earned my PhD in Biomedical Engineering in Italy as a **Marie Skłodowska-Curie Research Fellow** at CNR-IBB (Turin).

## 🔬 Research interests

<div class="research-grid" markdown="1">

- **CEST & acidoCEST MRI** — non-invasive tumor pH and metabolism imaging
- **MR Fingerprinting (MRF)** — quantitative multi-parametric mapping, DCE-MRF
- **MRI reporter genes** — tracking cell- and virus-based therapies *in vivo*
- **PET/MRI** — hybrid imaging of the tumor microenvironment
- **Deep learning for MRI** — reconstruction, quantification, dictionary matching
- **Pulse-sequence programming** — Bruker ParaVision & GE EPIC

</div>

## 📍 Currently

At **MGH / Harvard Medical School**, I'm developing and optimizing MRI reporter genes and quantitative CEST MRI detection methods for tracking oncolytic viruses and cell therapies in preclinical tumor models, with a focus on **deep learning-based CEST MR Fingerprinting** for imaging reporter proteins.
