### Hypothesis
Oral curcumin supplementation will reduce pro-inflammatory cytokine burden and clinical mucositis severity in patients undergoing melphalan-based conditioning for autologous stem-cell transplant.

### Approach
- Clinical PK-PD study in autologous transplant patients receiving melphalan conditioning.
- Serial sampling for curcumin PK and cytokines associated with mucositis.
- Correlation of exposures with clinical mucositis grading.

### Key findings / conclusion
Curcumin exposure was associated with attenuated inflammatory cytokine responses and a signal toward reduced mucositis severity, supporting further evaluation as a supportive-care agent in transplant conditioning.
