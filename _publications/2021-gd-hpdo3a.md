### Hypothesis
Appending an albumin-binding moiety to the Gd-HPDO3A chelate will prolong vascular residence, providing a safer alternative to linear blood-pool agents while retaining the macrocyclic stability of HPDO3A.

### Approach
- Synthesis of albumin-binding Gd-HPDO3A derivatives.
- Relaxometric characterization with and without albumin.
- *In vivo* DCE-MRI comparison against reference blood-pool and extracellular agents.

### Key findings / conclusion
The new agent showed strong albumin binding, high r1 relaxivity in plasma, and prolonged intravascular retention suitable for DCE-MRI of vascular perfusion and permeability, while keeping a macrocyclic, kinetically inert Gd chelate.
