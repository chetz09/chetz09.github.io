### Hypothesis
Tumor extracellular pH measured by *in vivo* MRI-CEST imaging is a sensitive biomarker for detecting resistance to proton pump inhibitor (PPI) therapy in human prostate cancer murine models.

### Approach
- PPI-sensitive and PPI-resistant human prostate cancer xenografts in mice.
- PPI treatment with longitudinal MRI-CEST pH imaging.
- Comparison of pH trajectories across responder vs non-responder tumors.

### Key findings / conclusion
Responder tumors showed the expected acidosis reversal after PPI treatment, while resistant tumors retained a low extracellular pH — validating MRI-CEST pH imaging as a non-invasive early marker of PPI resistance.
