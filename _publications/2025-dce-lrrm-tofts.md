### Hypothesis
The Linear Reference Region Model (LRRM) — which avoids explicit arterial input function measurement — will track drug-induced vascular disruption as well as, or better than, the conventional Tofts model in preclinical DCE-MRI.

### Approach
- DCE-MRI acquired before and after a vascular-disruption intervention in a preclinical tumor model.
- Same datasets fit with both the Tofts model (using measured AIF) and the LRRM (using a reference tissue).
- Comparison of quantitative parameters (Ktrans, ve, relative parameters) and their sensitivity to induced vascular disruption.

### Key findings / conclusion
LRRM provided perfusion estimates that were sensitive to vascular disruption and more robust to AIF-measurement error, suggesting it as a practical alternative to the Tofts model in preclinical longitudinal DCE-MRI studies.
