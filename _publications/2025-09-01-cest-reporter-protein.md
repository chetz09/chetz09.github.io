### Hypothesis
Introducing cation-pi interaction networks into CEST MRI reporter proteins will stabilize favorable proton-exchange environments and increase the measurable CEST contrast *in vitro* and *in vivo*.

### Approach
- Rational design of reporter protein variants incorporating cation-pi networks.
- Biophysical and CEST characterization of the designed variants.
- Comparison of contrast efficiency against parental reporter protein scaffolds.

### Key findings / conclusion
Cation-pi engineering yielded reporter variants with enhanced CEST contrast, providing a generalizable design principle for next-generation molecular-imaging reporters used to track cell- and virus-based therapies.
