### Hypothesis
Dynamic GlucoCEST MRI, which reports on glucose uptake via chemical-exchange saturation transfer, can detect early therapy response in triple-negative breast cancer comparably to 18F-FDG-PET — without ionizing radiation.

### Approach
- 4T1 murine TNBC model treated with chemotherapeutic and metabolic agents.
- Same-day GlucoCEST MRI and 18F-FDG-PET imaging before and after treatment.
- Voxelwise and volume-of-interest comparison of the two modalities.

### Key findings / conclusion
GlucoCEST changes correlated with FDG-PET response and with tumor-growth outcomes, supporting GlucoCEST as a practical, radiation-free alternative for monitoring metabolic response in TNBC.
