### Hypothesis
Pharmacological inhibition of proton transporters will raise tumor extracellular pH in a triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) murine model, and this change can be monitored non-invasively using MRI-CEST tumor pH imaging.

### Approach
- Established TNBC murine tumors (4T1).
- Treatment with proton pump / proton transport inhibitors alongside vehicle controls.
- Longitudinal MRI-CEST pH imaging with an iopamidol-based contrast agent.
- Correlation of imaging-derived pH with tumor growth and *ex vivo* readouts.

### Key findings / conclusion
MRI-CEST pH imaging detected drug-induced changes in tumor extracellular pH, correlating with therapeutic response — supporting CEST pH imaging as a non-invasive pharmacodynamic biomarker for metabolism-targeted therapies in TNBC.
