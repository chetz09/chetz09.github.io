### Hypothesis
An MR-Fingerprinting acquisition combined with a dynamic contrast-enhanced readout (DCE-MRF) can deliver reproducible, model-based estimates of tumor vascular perfusion parameters in a single scan.

### Approach
- Design of the DCE-MRF pulse-sequence schedule and matched dictionary.
- Phantom validation of quantitative accuracy.
- *In vivo* assessment of tumor vascular perfusion in a preclinical breast-cancer model.

### Key findings / conclusion
DCE-MRF produced reliable perfusion quantification with improved reproducibility compared with standard DCE-MRI, establishing it as a quantitative method suitable for longitudinal perfusion imaging.
