### Hypothesis
DCE-MRF — a rapid, dictionary-based multi-parametric acquisition — can detect *induced* tumor-vascular perfusion reductions more reliably than standard DCE-MRI analysis in a mouse breast cancer model.

### Approach
- Orthotopic mouse breast tumor model with pharmacologically induced vascular perfusion reduction.
- DCE-MRF acquisition compared head-to-head with conventional DCE-MRI on the same cohort.
- Quantitative perfusion parameters estimated via dictionary matching and compared statistically across control and treatment groups.

### Key findings / conclusion
DCE-MRF showed improved precision and higher sensitivity for detecting induced perfusion changes than conventional DCE-MRI, supporting DCE-MRF as a quantitative imaging biomarker of tumor vascular response in preclinical drug studies — and a promising route toward clinical translation.
